How to Check Scholarship Status in India

Last updated: June 2026

After submitting a scholarship application, students should regularly check their application status. Many applications need institute verification, district/state verification, document correction, or payment processing.

Status names can differ between portals such as NSP, Jnanabhumi, Telangana ePASS, AICTE, university portals, and private scholarship websites. Always check the official portal for the final and correct status.

ApplyMate India is only an informational student helper. It cannot approve, reject, or change any scholarship application.

Common scholarship status meanings

  • Submitted: Your application was submitted successfully.
  • Pending at Institute: Your school/college needs to verify your application.
  • Under Verification: Your application is being checked by officials.
  • Approved: Your application passed verification stage.
  • Rejected: Your application did not meet rules or had document/detail issues.
  • Payment Processing: Scholarship amount may be under payment transfer process.
  • Payment Released: Amount may have been sent to the linked bank account.

How to check status

  1. Open the official scholarship portal.
  2. Login using your registered details.
  3. Go to application status, track status, or student dashboard section.
  4. Check whether your application is pending, verified, approved, rejected, or sent for payment.
  5. Download or screenshot important acknowledgement/status details.
  6. Contact your institution if the application is pending at school/college level.

What to do if application is pending

If your scholarship is pending at institute level, contact your school or college scholarship section. Some students miss deadlines because they submit the form but forget institute verification.

What to do if rejected

  • Check the rejection reason carefully.
  • Verify name, Aadhaar, bank account, income certificate, and category certificate details.
  • Check whether your income exceeds the allowed limit.
  • Check whether you selected the correct scheme.
  • If correction window is open, correct and resubmit quickly.
  • If correction is not available, contact official helpdesk or institution.

Common tracking mistakes

  • Only submitting form but not checking verification status.
  • Using wrong login details or old application ID.
  • Not checking payment status after approval.
  • Ignoring correction messages.
  • Not contacting college when status is pending at institute.
