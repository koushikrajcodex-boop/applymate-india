Scholarship Mistakes Students Should Avoid

Last updated: June 2026

Many students lose scholarship opportunities not because they are ineligible, but because of simple mistakes during registration, document upload, application submission, or verification.

This guide explains common scholarship mistakes and how students can avoid them before applying on official portals such as NSP, Jnanabhumi, Telangana ePASS, AICTE, university portals, and private scholarship websites.

ApplyMate India is an informational student helper. Always verify final application rules, deadlines, and required documents on the official scholarship portal.

1. Waiting until the last date

Scholarship portals can become slow near deadlines. Students may also need time for document corrections, institute verification, or bank detail updates. Apply early whenever possible.

2. Entering name differently from documents

Your name should match your official documents such as Aadhaar, marks memo, school or college records, and bank account details where required. Small spelling differences can create verification problems.

3. Uploading unclear documents

Blurry images, cropped certificates, wrong file formats, or unreadable documents may lead to rejection or correction requests.

4. Selecting the wrong scholarship scheme

Some scholarships are only for specific states, categories, gender, courses, income groups, or education levels. Read the scheme guidelines carefully before applying.

5. Ignoring income limit rules

Many scholarships have strict annual family income limits. Students should check whether the income certificate amount fits the scheme rules before applying.

6. Wrong bank account details

Incorrect account number, IFSC code, inactive bank account, or mismatch in account holder details can delay scholarship payments.

7. Forgetting institute verification

Some scholarship applications must be verified by school, college, or institution. Submitting the form alone may not be enough. Students should track the status after submission.

8. Not saving acknowledgement details

After submission, save your application ID, acknowledgement number, login details, and screenshots of important status pages. These may help during tracking or support requests.

9. Using inactive mobile number or email

Scholarship portals may send OTPs, correction notices, status updates, or login information to your registered mobile number or email. Use details you can access.

10. Not checking correction window

If an application has errors, some portals may provide a correction window. Missing that window may result in rejection or delay.

Quick checklist before applying

  • Check official eligibility rules.
  • Check deadline and verification deadline.
  • Keep Aadhaar, income certificate, marks memo, and bank details ready.
  • Use correct name and date of birth.
  • Upload clear documents.
  • Save application ID after submission.
  • Track application status regularly.
  • Contact institution if verification is pending.

FAQs

What is the biggest scholarship application mistake?

One major scholarship application mistake is waiting until the last date. Students should apply early because portals may slow down and documents or verification steps may take extra time.

Why do scholarship applications get rejected?

Scholarship applications may be rejected because of wrong documents, spelling mismatch, incorrect bank details, wrong scheme selection, income limit issues, or missed verification steps.

How can students avoid scholarship delays?

Students can avoid delays by checking official eligibility rules, uploading clear documents, using correct details, saving acknowledgement numbers, and tracking institute verification status regularly.
