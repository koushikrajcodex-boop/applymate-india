Scholarship Guides for Indian Students

Last updated: 26 June 2026

This page explains common scholarship options and document requirements in simple language. The goal is to help students understand where to start before visiting the official application portal.

Scholarship rules can change. Always verify the latest eligibility, income limits, dates, and documents on the official portal before applying.

NSP Scholarship Guide

The National Scholarship Portal is used for many central and state scholarship schemes. Students should check scheme eligibility, application dates, institution verification requirements, and Aadhaar/bank details before applying.

AP Jnanabhumi Guide

Andhra Pradesh students applying for post-matric scholarships should check category rules, income certificate requirements, college details, previous marks memos, and official Jnanabhumi updates.

Telangana ePASS Guide

Telangana ePASS is commonly used for post-matric scholarship applications. Students should keep caste/category certificate, income certificate, Aadhaar, bank passbook, and college bonafide ready.

Engineering Scholarships

Engineering students may be eligible for national, state, category-based, girl student, disability, and merit-based scholarships. Always check whether your college/course is recognized by the scheme.

1. How to use ApplyMate India

  1. Select your state or choose National/Any State.
  2. Select your education level, such as school, degree, engineering, or post graduation.
  3. Select your category or special group.
  4. Select your annual family income range.
  5. Check possible scholarship matches and open the official portal for final verification.

2. Common documents needed for scholarships

Different schemes ask for different documents, but students are often asked to keep the following ready:

  • Aadhaar card or Aadhaar enrolment details
  • Income certificate
  • Caste/category certificate, if applicable
  • Disability certificate, if applicable
  • Bank passbook or bank account details
  • Previous marks memo
  • Bonafide certificate from school or college
  • Admission proof or fee receipt, if required
  • Passport-size photograph

3. NSP Scholarship Guide

The National Scholarship Portal, commonly called NSP, is a common platform where students can find and apply for several scholarship schemes. Students should read the scheme guidelines carefully before applying. Important details usually include income limit, eligible courses, eligible categories, required documents, institution verification, and application deadline.

Before applying through NSP, students should confirm that their Aadhaar details, bank account details, institution details, and previous academic records are correct. Small mistakes can delay verification or cause application rejection.

4. AP Jnanabhumi Scholarship Guide

Andhra Pradesh students often use Jnanabhumi for post-matric scholarship-related services. Students should verify the current scholarship year, eligible categories, college details, income limit, attendance rules, and document requirements on the official portal.

Common documents may include Aadhaar, income certificate, caste certificate, previous marks memo, bank passbook, and college bonafide certificate. Since rules may vary by welfare department and category, final confirmation should always be done on the official portal.

5. Telangana ePASS Scholarship Guide

Telangana ePASS is used for several student welfare scholarship applications. Students applying through ePASS should check their category, income eligibility, education level, and institution details.

Many applications require Aadhaar, income certificate, caste/category certificate, SSC memo, previous marks memo, bank account details, college bonafide certificate, and photograph. Students should verify the latest checklist before applying.

6. Scholarships for engineering students

Engineering students should look for multiple scholarship types: state post-matric scholarships, central scholarships, AICTE-related schemes, category-based scholarships, girl student scholarships, disability scholarships, and private merit scholarships.

Before applying, confirm whether your course, college, and admission type are accepted by the scholarship scheme. Some schemes may require admission in an approved institution or a specific type of course.

7. Scholarships for girl students

Girl students may be eligible for special scholarships that support technical education, higher education, or merit-based progress. While applying, check whether the scheme has conditions related to course type, family income, number of eligible children, institution approval, or admission year.

8. Common mistakes to avoid

  • Entering wrong bank account details
  • Using expired or incorrect income certificate
  • Uploading unclear document photos
  • Missing the deadline
  • Selecting the wrong category or course
  • Not checking whether the institution must verify the application
  • Assuming eligibility without reading official guidelines

9. Final reminder

ApplyMate India is a starting point, not a final authority. Use this app to discover possible options, then always verify details on the official scholarship portal before applying.

ApplyMate India is an informational tool. Scholarship rules, income limits, deadlines, and required documents may change. Always verify on the official portal before applying.
