NSP OTR Guide for Students

Last updated: June 2026

NSP OTR usually refers to One Time Registration on the National Scholarship Portal. It helps students create a common registration identity before applying for eligible scholarship schemes.

This guide explains the basic preparation steps. Final instructions, Aadhaar rules, mobile verification, application deadlines, and scheme requirements must always be checked on the official NSP portal.

ApplyMate India is not an official NSP website. Always verify final details at the official portal before applying.

Why NSP OTR is important

Many students apply for central or state scholarship schemes through NSP. OTR helps connect student details with scholarship applications and reduces repeated registration confusion.

Details students should keep ready

  • Aadhaar details, where required
  • Mobile number linked with student or family access
  • Email ID
  • Student name as per documents
  • Date of birth
  • Bank account details
  • School or college details
  • Income certificate
  • Caste/category/community certificate, if applicable
  • Previous academic marks memo

Common mistakes to avoid

  • Entering a different spelling of your name from Aadhaar or school records
  • Using an inactive mobile number
  • Uploading unclear documents
  • Selecting the wrong scheme
  • Waiting until the last date
  • Not checking institute verification status

After OTR, what next?

  1. Login to the official portal.
  2. Check eligible scholarship schemes.
  3. Read scheme guidelines carefully.
  4. Fill the application form.
  5. Upload required documents.
  6. Submit and save acknowledgement details.
  7. Track application and verification status regularly.
