AP Jnanabhumi Scholarship Guide

Last updated: 26 June 2026

Jnanabhumi is commonly used by Andhra Pradesh students for scholarship-related services, especially post-matric scholarship support. Students should verify their category, course, college, income limit, and document requirements before applying.

ApplyMate India is not an official Andhra Pradesh government portal. Use this guide only as a starting point.

Who should check Jnanabhumi?

  • Intermediate students in Andhra Pradesh
  • Degree, engineering, diploma, and PG students
  • Students from eligible welfare categories
  • Students whose college participates in the official scholarship process

Common documents needed

  • Aadhaar card
  • Income certificate
  • Caste/category certificate if applicable
  • Bank passbook
  • Previous marks memo
  • College bonafide certificate
  • Attendance proof if required
  • Fee receipt if required

Application checks

  1. Check whether your course and college are eligible.
  2. Verify the correct welfare department/category rules.
  3. Keep Aadhaar, bank, income, and category details ready.
  4. Confirm whether your college needs to verify your application.
  5. Track application status on the official portal.

Common mistakes to avoid

  • Using old or invalid income certificate
  • Entering wrong bank account details
  • Name mismatch in Aadhaar, bank, or certificates
  • Not checking college verification status
  • Assuming the same income limit applies to every category

Official portal

Verify final details here: Jnanabhumi Official Portal.

FAQ

Does ApplyMate India guarantee Jnanabhumi scholarship approval?

No. Approval depends on official rules, documents, verification, and eligibility.

Can engineering students check Jnanabhumi?

Yes, eligible engineering students in Andhra Pradesh may need to check Jnanabhumi or related official instructions.

Should I verify on the official portal?

Yes. Always verify current rules and deadlines on the official Jnanabhumi portal.
