Documents Needed for Scholarship Applications

Last updated: 26 June 2026

Scholarship applications often require documents to prove identity, education, income, category, bank details, and institution details. The exact list changes by scheme, so students should always verify the official instructions.

This is a general checklist. Final document requirements depend on the official scholarship scheme.

Common document checklist

  • Aadhaar card or Aadhaar enrolment details
  • Income certificate
  • Caste/category certificate if applicable
  • Disability certificate if applicable
  • Bank passbook or bank account details
  • Previous marks memo
  • School or college bonafide certificate
  • Admission proof
  • Fee receipt if required
  • Passport-size photograph

Identity documents

Aadhaar is commonly requested in many scholarship applications. Students should make sure their name and details are correct and match other documents wherever required.

Income and category documents

Many scholarships require income certificates. Category-based scholarships may also require caste, category, minority, disability, or related certificates. These documents should be valid and clearly readable.

Education documents

Marks memos, admission proof, bonafide certificates, and fee receipts may be required to prove current education status. Students should collect these from their school, college, or institution before the application deadline.

Bank documents

Bank passbook or account details may be required for scholarship payment. Wrong bank details can delay or affect payment, so students should enter account number and IFSC carefully.

Tips before uploading documents

  • Make sure photos or scans are clear.
  • Check spelling of name and Aadhaar details.
  • Use valid certificates.
  • Keep file size and format as required by the official portal.
  • Do not wait until the last date.

FAQ

Is income certificate always needed?

Many scholarships need it, but not all. Check the specific scheme guidelines.

Can unclear documents cause rejection?

Unclear or incorrect documents may delay verification or affect application status.

Should I upload documents from unofficial sources?

No. Use official and valid documents issued by the proper authority or institution.
