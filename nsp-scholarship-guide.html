NSP Scholarship Guide for Indian Students

Last updated: 26 June 2026

The National Scholarship Portal, also called NSP, is a common platform where students can find and apply for many scholarship schemes. It can include central schemes, state schemes, and schemes from different departments.

Who should check NSP?

  • School students looking for pre-matric or merit-based support
  • Intermediate, degree, engineering, and PG students
  • Students from eligible categories such as SC, ST, OBC, minority, EWS, or disability groups
  • Students looking for central or state scholarship schemes

Common documents needed

  • Aadhaar card or Aadhaar enrolment details
  • Bank account details
  • Income certificate if required
  • Caste/category certificate if applicable
  • Previous marks memo
  • Bonafide certificate from institution
  • Passport-size photograph

Step-by-step process

  1. Visit the official NSP website.
  2. Check the scheme list and eligibility rules.
  3. Register or log in as per current instructions.
  4. Fill student, education, bank, and institution details carefully.
  5. Upload documents if required.
  6. Submit and track verification status.

Common mistakes

  • Entering wrong bank account details
  • Selecting the wrong scheme or category
  • Uploading unclear documents
  • Missing institution verification
  • Not checking the deadline

Official portal

Always verify details on the official portal: National Scholarship Portal.

FAQ

Is ApplyMate India an official NSP portal?

No. ApplyMate India is only an informational tool.

Can eligibility change?

Yes. Scholarship eligibility, deadlines, and required documents can change. Always check the official portal.
