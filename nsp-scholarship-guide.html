NSP Scholarship Guide for Indian Students

Last updated: 26 June 2026

The National Scholarship Portal, also called NSP, is one of the most important scholarship platforms for Indian students. It can include central schemes, state schemes, ministry schemes, and other student financial support programs.

This guide helps students understand basic NSP preparation, documents, common mistakes, and official verification steps before applying for scholarships.

ApplyMate India is not an official NSP portal. Always verify final eligibility, deadlines, documents, and application steps on the official National Scholarship Portal before applying.

Who should check NSP?

  • School students looking for pre-matric or merit-based scholarships
  • Intermediate, degree, engineering, diploma, and postgraduate students
  • Students from eligible categories such as SC, ST, OBC, EBC, EWS, minority, or disability groups
  • Students looking for central or state scholarship schemes
  • Students who want to track scholarship application status on the official portal

Common documents needed

  • Aadhaar card or Aadhaar enrolment details
  • Mobile number and email ID
  • Bank account details
  • Income certificate if required
  • Caste, category, or community certificate if applicable
  • Disability certificate if applicable
  • Previous marks memo
  • Current course or admission proof
  • Bonafide certificate from institution
  • Passport-size photograph if required

Step-by-step preparation

  1. Visit the official National Scholarship Portal.
  2. Check the available scheme list carefully.
  3. Read eligibility rules before registration or login.
  4. Keep Aadhaar, mobile number, bank, education, and certificate details ready.
  5. Register or log in as per the current portal instructions.
  6. Fill student, education, bank, and institution details carefully.
  7. Upload documents only if required by the scheme.
  8. Submit the application and save acknowledgement details.
  9. Track institute verification and application status regularly.

Common mistakes to avoid

  • Entering incorrect bank account details
  • Using a name spelling that does not match Aadhaar or school records
  • Selecting the wrong scholarship scheme or category
  • Uploading unclear or expired documents
  • Missing institution verification
  • Applying after the deadline
  • Assuming old rules are still valid without checking the official portal

Official portal

Verify final details here: National Scholarship Portal.

FAQ

Is ApplyMate India an official NSP website?

No. ApplyMate India is only an informational student-help website. Final application must be done through the official portal.

Can engineering students apply through NSP?

Some engineering-related scholarships may be available through NSP depending on scheme rules and eligibility.

Can eligibility change every year?

Yes. Scholarship eligibility, income limits, document requirements, and deadlines may change.

Where should I verify final details?

Always verify final details on the official National Scholarship Portal before applying.
