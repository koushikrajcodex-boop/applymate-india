Engineering Scholarships in India

Last updated: 26 June 2026

Engineering education can be expensive, especially when students need to pay tuition fees, hostel fees, transport, books, exam fees, and other study-related costs. Scholarships can reduce financial pressure for eligible students.

This guide helps engineering students understand common scholarship categories, eligibility checks, required documents, and mistakes to avoid before applying through official scholarship portals.

Scholarship rules vary by scheme, state, category, college, course, and academic year. Always verify final eligibility, income limits, deadlines, and documents on the official portal.

Types of scholarships engineering students should check

  • State post-matric scholarships
  • National Scholarship Portal schemes
  • AICTE-related scholarships
  • Girl student scholarships
  • Disability scholarships
  • SC, ST, OBC, EBC, and EWS category scholarships
  • Minority scholarships
  • Private merit-based scholarships
  • University or college-level scholarships

Common eligibility checks

  • Course must be eligible under the scheme
  • Institution may need approval, recognition, or correct portal registration
  • Student category may matter
  • Family income limit may apply
  • Previous academic performance may be checked
  • Admission year or current year of study may matter
  • Application must be submitted before the deadline

Documents commonly needed

  • Aadhaar card
  • Admission proof
  • Previous marks memo
  • Income certificate
  • Category certificate if applicable
  • Disability certificate if applicable
  • Bank passbook or bank account details
  • College bonafide certificate
  • Fee receipt if required
  • Passport-size photo if required

Before applying

Engineering students should check whether their college, course, admission year, and institution approval status are accepted by the scholarship scheme. Students should also confirm whether renewal is required every academic year.

Common mistakes

  • Applying for a scholarship that does not support the student's course
  • Using incorrect income, category, or college details
  • Missing renewal deadlines
  • Not checking whether the college has verified the application
  • Uploading unclear documents
  • Ignoring official portal updates or correction windows

FAQ

Can private college students apply for scholarships?

Sometimes yes, but it depends on the scholarship scheme and institution eligibility.

Is income certificate always required?

Many scholarships require it, but rules vary by scheme. Always check the official notification.

Can first-year engineering students apply?

Many schemes allow eligible first-year students, but students must check scheme-specific rules.

Can engineering students use NSP?

Some engineering-related scholarships may be available through NSP depending on current scheme rules.
